Abstract¶

The Israel-Palestine conflict is one deeply rooted in historical, religious and political roots and has had long lasting effects on todays society. It traces its origins to the late 19th century when nationalist movements emerged among Jews and Arabs in response to the decline of the Ottoman Empire. The conflict gained momentum with the Zionist movement's call for a Jewish homeland in Palestine, leading to tensions over land and identity.

In 1946, The United Nations proposed a partition plan to establish separate Jewish and Arab states, but it was met with opposition from Arab nations. The subsequent establishment of the State of Israel in 1948 led to armed conflicts, creating a pattern of wars and hostilities over the ensuing decades. The status of Jerusalem, the right of return for Palestinian refugees, and the establishment of a Palestinian state are key unresolved issues fueling the conflict.

throughout the years, there have been mass casualities on both sides, resulting in horrible tragedy and loss for both the people of Palestine and Israel. It is essential to understand the casualties in the conflict when we try to look at it from a neutral perspective, through this analysis, we can understand the great disparities on both sides.

Unfortunately, keeping track of people related data can become complicated in large scale conflicts such as this one, but by using data science, we can take big sets and make them readable and understandable to us.

Methodology¶

I started by trying to locate a source code I then found a dataset for deaths in the conflict from 2000-2023. It included essential data such as: Name of victim, age, date of event, date of death, event location, (reigon, district), gender, combatant or non compatant, place of residence,type of injury, ammunition, killed by, and a notes section. I then searched for source code for the dataset and found one that used matplotlib to plot these numbers on different graphs. To modify the source code, I added a pie chart to display the information in another manner.

Conclusion¶

In conclusion, this conflict is one that has been demonstrated to be extremely deadly and volatile. More than just deadly, there is a clear inequality in the deaths and wounded. Palestinian people were shown to suffer much more than israeli people, prompting a conversation where we can discuss why these inequalities exist. In these types of global conflicts, it is important to mantain a good understanding of where we stand on the matter, and python has proved to be an extremely effective tool in data science and overall data visualization.
